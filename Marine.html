What is Marine Diversity?

explains what marine diversity is

Marine biodiversity is the variety of life in our ocean. It includes all animals, plants and microorganisms living in our ocean, from barnacles to whales to coral reefs. The term is also used to describe the abundance of species living in an area.

What harms marine diversity?

The amazing variety of life in our oceans is under threat. Marine animals are getting entangled in plastic debris, mistaking it for food or getting trapped. Pollution from land runs into the ocean, harming ecosystems and creatures. This loss of marine diversity disrupts the delicate balance of the ocean, affecting everything from the fish we eat to the very health of our planet.

How can we preserve Marine biodiversity?

The future of our ocean's astounding variety of life depends on our actions. By making small changes in our daily habits, we can collectively make a big difference. Supporting sustainable seafood choices through certifications like the MSC label reduces pressure on fish populations. Saying no to single-use plastics and properly disposing of waste helps keep our oceans clean. Advocating for policies that limit overfishing and protect marine habitats safeguards ecosystems for future generations. Joining beach clean-up efforts and raising awareness about marine conservation all contribute to a healthier ocean teeming with life.
